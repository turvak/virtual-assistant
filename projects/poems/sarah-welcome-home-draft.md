## Final Draft

**Welcome Home**

She survived five days of camp — in cabins, not a tent —
She herded neuro-diverse kids through every waking event.
She swims the Waiake ocean before most people are awake,
She trains in the garage, then drives a bus. For goodness' sake.

She was acting principal last year, now Deputy again,
She holds the crumbling school together while I hold the remote in vain.
Each morning, oat milk cappuccinos — hers and mine, no less —
Each night, red bush tea, magnesium, collagen, and the stress.

But Wednesday ran just fine without her — mostly — sort of — look:
Jax did rugby, then did band; the kids got fed, I cooked.
The takeaways were briefly weighed and reasonably declined.
The house was functional. The couch survived. We all were fine.

She'll fall asleep before the show ends. That part I know.
But home's a lot more home when she's in it — just so.
